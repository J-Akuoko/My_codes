Principal Investigator

Dr Lin Ma is a lecturer (equivalent to assistant professor) focusing on Multi-scale imaging and characterization of rocks (Digital rocks) in geoenergy reservoirs and subsurface energy storage. She is the Academic lead/co-chair of Women@manchester staff network (with > 600 members) at the university, and leading on subsurface energy and storage at University of Manchester at Harwell. Her current research aims to build novel and advanced 3D/4D digital approaches and correlative experiments to fully understand the complicated pore networks and the reactions of subsurface energy (e.g. natural gas, geothermal, hydrogen and CO2) with rocks under subsurface conditions (e.g. temperature, pressure, and chemistry) across multiple scales (nm to cm).

PhD Student

Mr Jeffrey Akuoko holds an MSc in Oil and Gas Engineering from the University of Aberdeen (Scotland), and a BSc in Geomatic Engineering from the University of Mines and Technology (Ghana). He is currently working on integrating experimental methods, deep learning, advanced imaging and analytical techniques, and thermodynamic modelling to understand mechanisms behind water iron-rich rock reactions for hydrogen storage and carbon sequestration.
